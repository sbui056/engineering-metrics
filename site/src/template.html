Who does the codebase
depend on?

Impact is the degree to which an engineer's work is depended on, trusted, and hard to replace — measured from what survived, what it's coupled to, and who trusts whom to review. Not from commit counts.

impact = 0.25·pct(ownership) + 0.25·pct(survival) + 0.25·pct(coupling) + 0.25·pct(reviews)

Method & caveats

The four signals

Ownership concentration
Size-weighted blame-share over files where the author is a major owner (≥5% ownership plus an absolute floor).
Code survival
Surviving lines vs. lines added, tenure-normalized; recent additions are excluded as too young to judge.
Coupling criticality
PageRank centrality of files in the co-change graph, weighted by the author's blame share — the criticality of what they own, not what they touched.
Review leverage
Distinct authors reviewed × log(1 + reviews given). Reach, not depth.

Scoring

Each signal is percentile-normalized over this repo's contributors and combined with equal weights. Equal weights are a deliberate, transparent choice, not a calibrated one — with no ground truth at this sample size, unit weights are hard to beat (Dawes 1979). Missing review data is imputed to the median, never to zero, so a data gap can't masquerade as "worst"; imputed rows carry a ◌ no review data badge.

Limits — read before quoting a number

  • Blame only sees lines that survived to HEAD: it favors code that hasn't been rewritten and undercounts foundational-but-refactored work.
  • Coupling has a cold-start blind spot — a load-bearing but stable file scores low — and can reward entanglement, which is sometimes a design smell.
  • Ownership concentration is dual-use: "hard to replace" and "bus-factor risk". Scored positively, always flagged, never silently rewarded.
  • Owned-criticality and ownership both scale with blame share, so ownership carries more than its nominal 25% — the four signals are really ~three independent dimensions. A conscious trade-off.
  • Review leverage measures reach, not depth: a one-line LGTM counts like a substantive review.
  • Every signal is a gameable proxy (Goodhart): publishing this formula as a target would erode it — territorial hoarding, split PRs, rubber stamps.
  • Not measured at all: mentoring, design, incident response, unblocking others, and any off-repo work.
  • Raw commit count and lines-of-code remain rejected baselines, shown above only as a contrast, never scored.
